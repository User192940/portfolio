03 Research and Documentation

CSS Specificity

What is Specificity?

CSS specificity refers to a calculated number that determines what style the browser will end up using. Higher specificity values mean it is more important.

Contrast Ratios

A contrast ratio is the ratio of brightness between the brightest shade and the darkest shade. In the real world, it tells us the difference between two colors. If the number is low then the colors are closer in appearance. If it is higher then they are more different in appearance.

Importance of Contrast Ratios

Contrast ratios are important to pay attention to for multiple reasons. First, they affect the appearance of a site. Usually, a higher contrast ratio between the background and text is more visually appealing. Second, many people are visually impaired and cannot tell the difference between low-contrast ratio colors. If you fail to meet the legal standard you or your company can be sued.

Who Sets Accessibility Rules?

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines are developed by the Accessibility Guidelines Working Group which is a part of the W3C.

Summary

There are many things to keep in mind when making websites. In this documentation, I covered CSS specificity, contrast ratios, and who set the accessibility rules.
