WordPress

WordPress History

WordPress was made in 2003 from a platform called Cafelog/b2. Its goals were user experience, extensibility, and adaptability. 1.0 introduced permalinks, multiple categories, search engine-friendly urls and plugins. Shortly after that, themes and expansions were added. From then on it got better and better. Today WordPress is the most used CMS. Over 40% of websites use WordPress.

WordPress.org Entity

The WordPress.org entity is dedicated to keeping everything open source. It provides source files for WordPress, forums, downloads, tools, and documentation. It is the heart of the WordPress community and embodies open-source software, collaboration, and democratizing content publishing on the web.

WordPress.com Entity

WordPress.com is a platform with a business model. They offer hosting, maintenance, and support to those who aren't tech-savvy at a monthly cost. The price of services depend on what your organization needs.

Summary

WordPress is a CMS that was founded in 2003. It is the most popular CMS on the market, powering over 40% of websites. WordPress.org is the central hub for all things that WordPress stands for. It has open-source software, forums, tools, and documentation. WordPress.com is a commercial platform that offers hosted websites, where users can create websites without worrying about maintenance, hosting, and updates.
